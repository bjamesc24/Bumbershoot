## 1. Project Overview

Project Bumbershoot Collaboration is a capstone development initiative to design and deliver a stable, customer-service-grade mobile festival companion application for the Bumbershoot music and arts festival. The project focuses on reliability, usability, and operational resilience in live festival conditions, prioritizing predictable behavior and offline capability over feature breadth.

The project outcome is a native mobile application that supports attendee discovery, planning, navigation, and communication using official festival data.

---

## 2. Business Problem

Large-scale festivals such as Bumbershoot operate in environments that are inherently hostile to mobile applications. High user density, intermittent network connectivity, frequent schedule adjustments, and time-sensitive decision-making create friction for attendees attempting to navigate programming, venues, and services.

Without a reliable digital companion, attendees rely on fragmented sources of information, manual planning, or real-time social coordination that often fails under festival conditions. This results in confusion, missed performances, reduced confidence in official information, and increased operational strain on festival staff.

---

## 3. Business Narrative

Bumbershoot requires a dependable digital platform that functions as an authoritative, attendee-facing companion throughout the festival experience. Rather than competing on feature richness, the application must succeed by being predictable, fast, and resilient — even when connectivity is limited or unavailable.

The system acts as a calm, trustworthy guide that helps attendees make informed decisions, adapt to changes, and navigate the festival grounds without requiring setup, accounts, or continuous network access. Stability is treated as a core product feature, not a secondary quality.

## 4. Project Goals and Success Criteria

### 4.1 Project Goals

- Deliver a stable, production-minded mobile application suitable for live festival use
- Enable attendees to discover events, artists, and venues reliably
- Support personal planning through local favorites, timelines, and reminders
- Provide navigation and operational information with minimal network dependency
- Demonstrate professional-grade requirements definition, architecture, and execution

### 4.2 Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful when:

- Core system capabilities defined in the SRS are implemented or formally deferred
- The application remains usable under offline or degraded network conditions
- Key user workflows perform consistently without crashes or blocking failures
- Stability and performance requirements defined in the SRS are validated
- The sponsor confirms alignment with stated goals and scope

---

## 5. High-Level Scope

### 5.1 In-Scope

- Native mobile festival companion application
- Schedule browsing and discovery (time, stage, category)
- Event, artist, and booth information
- Locally stored favorites and personal planning timeline
- Performance reminders and schedule change notifications
- Festival maps with venues and points of interest
- Festival announcements and operational service information
- External sharing of event and artist details
- Predictable data refresh and offline fallback behavior

### 5.2 Out-of-Scope

- User accounts, authentication, or identity management
- Cross-device data synchronization
- Real-time location tracking or live user positioning
- Social networking features (messaging, profiles, friend graphs)
- In-app ticket purchasing or payment processing
- Continuous real-time data polling
- Administrative content management interfaces
- Web-based or desktop client applications

## 8. Key Risks

- Schedule or content changes introduce unexpected data variability
- Network congestion impacts live data refresh
- Performance degradation due to large datasets
- Scope expansion threatens stability and delivery timelines

Mitigation strategies are addressed through stability-first design, explicit scope boundaries, and disciplined change control.

---

## 9. Constraints

- Fixed academic timeline and capstone delivery schedule
- Limited development resources and team size
- Sponsor-provided data availability and format
- Budget-conscious hosting and API usage expectations
- Compliance with Bellevue College policies and standards
